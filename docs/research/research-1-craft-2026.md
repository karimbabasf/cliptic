## 1. STRUCTURE & PACING

### 1.1 The 3-second audition (CONVERGENT, very strong)
- TikTok/Shorts algorithms gate distribution on **completion rate + rewatch**, not likes/followers. The first 3 seconds is "your audition for broader distribution." [1,5]
- First 3 seconds drive ~**80% of completion variance**. Most drop-off happens in seconds 0–3. [search:OpusClip/Terra]
- Videos with **>65% three-second retention get 4–7x more impressions** than videos that lose viewers immediately (data across 5k–500k follower accounts). [1]
- Videos retaining **60%+ past 3s** are far more likely to be pushed to FYP/Reels/Shorts shelf. [search]
- 2026 nuance: the bar is moving even earlier — several sources now say **hook within the first 0.5 seconds**. [5]

### 1.2 Hook construction
- Spoken hook should land its core message in **first 2–3 seconds ≈ 10–14 spoken words**. [search:OpusClip]
- On-screen hook TEXT (first frame): **4–7 words**, big and readable, designed to land with sound off. [search:Virvid/CapCut]
- **Layered hooks (visual + auditory + textual) boost 3-second holds ~3x** vs single-element intros (2026 analyses). [6,18]
- The "stopping element" (bold claim / question / visual interrupt) must hit in the first ~2 seconds; the next 3–5 seconds expand/add context. [1]
- Rotate **3–4 hook formulas**; reusing one formula causes viewer fatigue. [1]

**Top 2026 hook formulas (most cited):**
- Contrarian Claim ("Everything you've been told about X is wrong")
- Mistake Warning ("Stop doing X")
- List Tease ("3 things that…")
- Direct Promise / Outcome ("How to X in Y seconds")
- Question hook (relatability)
- Pattern Interrupt (visual/sound shock relevant to topic)
- Mid-action / in-medias-res ("start in the middle of a moment")
  Sources: [1,2,6,18]

**Niche → hook mapping** [2]:
- Educational/productivity/business → **Direct Promise / numbered** ("3 settings that fix blurry Shorts")
- Entertainment/comedy → **Pattern interrupt / unexpected opening**
- Lifestyle/vlog → **Question hooks** (relatability)
- Tech/tutorial → **Numbered hooks** ("The 1 edit that saves 10 minutes")
- Food/recipe → **immediate visual appeal + time promise** ("60-second pasta that actually tastes good")
- Storytelling → **mid-action hook**

### 1.3 Pacing / cut cadence (CONVERGENT directional)
- 2026 target: a **visual change every 1.5–2 seconds** for videos under 60s. [17, search:Aibrify]
- Stated as **5–7 "clicks"/cuts per 10 seconds** of visual stimulus density. [17, search]
- Place **at least one pattern interrupt within the first 2 seconds**, then periodically. [20]
- B-roll cadence in explanation-heavy stretches: **insert B-roll every 60–90s**; find any stretch >90s where visuals don't change and add a "B-roll breath." [17]
- WARNING (CONVERGENT): do not stack effects back-to-back. "Deliberate disruption, not digital whiplash." Over-interrupting dilutes effectiveness. [19,20]
- Claimed effect: pattern interrupts can **lift watch time up to 85%** and conversion ~32% (single-source, treat as marketing-directional). [search:LightningIM]

### 1.4 Length trends 2026 (CONVERGENT)
- Sweet spot for virality / highest completion: **21–34 seconds** (TikTok especially). [27,28,29]
- YouTube Shorts: top performers cluster **25–35s**; ~half of viral Shorts **20–40s**; average Short ≈ **33s** (Apr 2026). [27]
- Instagram Reels: algorithm favors **15–30s**. [28]
- TikTok educational content: an emerging longer band of **45–90s** is rewarded when retention holds. [5]
- General rule: under 60s, one message per video, clear hook in first 2s. [28]
- TikTok videos **under 30s get ~45% more completion** than longer clips (partly because they get rewatched). [search]

### 1.5 Loop / rewatch tactics (CONVERGENT, strong)
- **Average Percentage Viewed** is described as "the holy grail of the 2026 algorithm"; rewatch is the heaviest-weighted FYP signal. [24,26]
- Loop format = video (typically **under 15s**) engineered so the **last frame flows seamlessly into the first frame**, triggering involuntary replay (no conscious decision to rewatch). [24,25]
- Two failure points that break the loop: (1) a **visible jump cut** at the loop boundary; (2) an **audible audio cut** at the boundary. Both must be continuous. [24]
- Micro-preview tactic: open with a **0.3-second flash of the end result**, then cut back to the beginning — creates instant curiosity + sets up the loop. [2]

## 2. CAPTIONS AS A RETENTION TOOL

### 2.1 WHY captions boost watch time (CONVERGENT, strong)
- **~85% of social video is watched without sound** (range cited 50–85% across sources). Without captions you lose most viewers immediately in sound-off feeds. [3,5, search]
- **Captioned videos: ~40% higher completion rate** on average. [5]
- Well-STYLED captions specifically improve watch time **23–40%** (vs raw/auto captions). [4]
- Adding captions raises completion ~**32%**; TikToks with "watch till the end" in the caption see **~21% longer watch duration**; captioned Reels get **~25% more shares**. [search:Zebracat/MarketingLTB]
- Mechanism (the "feel"): word-by-word reveal **synced to speech creates a reading rhythm that locks the viewer in** — eyes track the highlighted word at the pace of speech, so they never "lose their place," and never have to choose between reading and watching. This reduces cognitive load and acts as a visual anchor. [3,4, search]
- Captions must be **burned in** (not relying on auto-generated platform text) for Reels priority. [5]

### 2.2 Reading speed & words-on-screen (CONVERGENT, strong — these are the load-bearing numbers)
- Mobile reading speed: **200–250 words/min ≈ 3–4 words/second**. [3,21,32]
- Ideal caption chunk: **3–7 words**, displayed **1–3 seconds**. [3]
- A **5-word caption should display ~1.5–2 seconds**. [3]
- Per-chunk display: **min 1s, max 6s; sweet spot 2–3s**. [3]
- Professional subtitle standard: **17 characters/second (CPS) comfortable**, up to **20 CPS** for experienced viewers (Netflix allows 20 CPS adult). [32, search:Subtitling.net]
- Line limits: **max 42 characters/line, max 2 lines** (3 lines blocks the picture and overwhelms). [21, search]
- For the fast-paced kinetic style specifically: hold **2–4 word chunks for ~600–900ms each** (the current "deliberate" pace). Cutting between words faster than ~200–240 wpm reads as cheap. [search:Blitzcut/VFXAI]
- Technical terms/numbers → hold slightly longer. Break at natural speech pauses/punctuation; never split adjective+noun or verb phrases. [3]

### 2.3 Timing to speech / sync (CONVERGENT)
- Captions should **appear just as the word is spoken, disappear shortly after**. Too early spoils the punchline; lagging forces re-reads and increases drop-off. [3]
- Best-practice lead: **caption should lead audio by 100–200 milliseconds** (text slightly ahead of the sound feels tighter). [4]
- Active-word highlight should advance **at the exact pace of speech** (karaoke/Hormozi style) so the eye is guided word-by-word. [3]

### 2.4 Placement / safe areas (CONVERGENT — critical, platform-specific)
- **Safest caption zone = center-middle to upper-middle** of the frame. Avoid **bottom 25%** (UI cluster) and **top 15%** (username). [3]
- Best-performing TikTok caption position in practice: **lower-middle third** (above the UI), centered. [9, search:Blitzcut]
- TikTok 9:16 (1080×1920) safe margins [13]:
  - Top: keep **≥130px** clear (~6.8% of 1920)
  - Bottom: keep **≥484px** clear (~25% of 1920) — the most critical zone (caption, music ticker, account name)
  - Right: keep **≥140px** clear (~13% of 1080) — right third is the engagement-button "death zone" for text
- Meta Reels 2026 unified safe zone [22,23]:
  - Top **14%** (≈270px), Bottom **35%** (≈670px), Sides **6%** (≈65px)
  - The 35% bottom margin is the #1 cause of "creative failure" (290px more than Stories)
- Obscured CTAs → **~40% higher bounce rate**. [search:1ClickReport]
- Dynamic placement (text moves to dodge on-screen action / for visual rhythm) is endorsed but costs editing time. [3]

### 2.5 Font / size / contrast (CONVERGENT)
- TikTok default that works: **white font, black stroke** — strongest contrast across backgrounds. Alt: yellow w/ black outline, or black text w/ white outline. [3,9]
- Font size for 9:16 1080×1920: **~55–75pt is common** for burned captions. (~5–7% of the 1080 width per cap-height; treat 6–8% of frame WIDTH as the practical cap-height target for body captions.) [search:Stackinfluence/Blitzcut]
- Always **bold/semi-bold weight**; sans-serif (Helvetica, Arial, Montserrat, Proxima/Inter family). Avoid decorative fonts. [3,10]
- **Contrast over aesthetics** — pastel fonts fail in sunlight/light backgrounds. Test on older/low-quality screens. [3]
- Emphasis (color/size/bold pop) used **sparingly**, only on key words/stats/punchlines — not constant noise. [3]

## 3. CURRENT AESTHETIC TRENDS 2026

### 3.1 What reads as CURRENT in 2026 [9,11,12,15,16, search:FontMirror]
Five typography moves that are now "default current":
1. **Word-by-word kinetic captions with keyword color pops** (one accent color on the key word only).
2. **Oversized centered hooks in heavy sans-serifs** (big bold opening title).
3. **Hand-written / marker-style annotation layers** (highlighter swipes, scribbled arrows).
4. **Mixed-case bold for emphasis instead of ALL-CAPS.**
5. **Animated text that scales/rotates in time with voiceover beats** (beat-synced motion).

- Current kinetic pace: **slower & deliberate — 2–4 word chunks held 600–900ms each.** [search]
- High-contrast color combos (white/black-outline; yellow/black-outline) remain the readable baseline. [9]

### 3.2 What reads as DATED / OVERUSED in 2026 (CONVERGENT, important)
- **Chunky Montserrat + yellow keyword highlight + slight bounce-in entry** — the "Hormozi preset." Became the default in nearly every AI tool by 2025, so it now reads as **generic/AI-default**, not distinctive. [search:FontMirror/Blitzcut]
- **Early/fast kinetic captions cutting between words at 200–240 wpm** read as cheap. [search]
- Over-maximalist 2024–2025 style (everything flashing, effect stacking) is on the way out; 2026 shift is toward **deliberate pacing + authentic-feeling overlays.** [search:OpusClip-trends/Blitzcut]
- Cross-posting with a visible TikTok watermark tanks Reels reach. [5]

### 3.3 EMERGING / counter-trend [search:FontMirror, 16]
- **Hand-drawn annotation layers** (wobbly yellow highlighter, scribbled circles/arrows) are rising. A slightly imperfect annotation signals "a real person decided this part mattered" → pulls perceived authenticity up even on AI-generated video.
- More restraint, more "human" imperfection, fewer simultaneous effects.

### 3.4 Niche style differences [2,4,9,12]
- **Talking-head / educational:** word-by-word presets show the highest retention; clean high-contrast captions, numbered/direct-promise hooks, punch-ins on key points. Longer 45–90s band acceptable.
- **Lifestyle / vlog:** softer, question-led hooks, relatability; captions can be lighter-touch, aesthetic-leaning but still readable.
- **Hype / finance / fitness / motivational:** **bold statement presets excel** for hook-driven/motivational clips; big oversized hook titles, aggressive emphasis pops, fast energy, beat-synced scale animation, transformation/proof-first openings (fitness).
- **Food/recipe:** immediate visual appeal + time-promise; captions support the action, often upper-third so the food stays visible.

## 4. ON-SCREEN ELEMENTS (zoom, emoji, B-roll, SFX pop-ups)

### 4.1 Zoom / punch-in [search:OpusClip/CapCut, 19]
- **Punch-in** = digital zoom on the speaker's face for emphasis at key points / credibility moments (great for B2B/educational).
- Use punch-ins as one of the "visual change every 1.5–2s" beats; keep them brief and tied to a specific emphasized word/claim.
- Rapid zoom is a proven OPENING pattern interrupt. [2]

### 4.2 Pattern interrupts that live in the edit [19,20]
- Quick cuts, unusual angles, high-contrast visuals, sudden color change, freeze-frame + text overlay, zoom-in. Movement triggers the peripheral-vision attention response before conscious processing.
- The interrupt must be **relevant to the content** — test the first 3s on MUTE: if the visual alone says nothing about the topic, it's purely decorative and won't hold. [1]

### 4.3 Emoji / stickers [3,5]
- Strategic emoji placement for **emotional emphasis** is a listed high-performing technique — but sparingly (key beats only), same rule as caption emphasis: enhance, don't add noise.

### 4.4 B-roll [17]
- Build a reusable B-roll library (cited as 3–5x editing speed-up). Insert every 60–90s in explanation-heavy sections; never let visuals sit static >90s.
- B-roll cutaways function as pattern interrupts and as "breaths."

### 4.5 "Sound-effect + image pop-up" moments — timing conventions (CONVERGENT, strong, very actionable) [30,31, search:Finchley]
- A well-timed **whoosh / ding / pop** on a text reveal, swipe, transition, or image pop-up = the difference between polished and amateur.
- **Frame-level precision required.** An effect that hits **3–4 frames late sounds amateur.** Always zoom to frame level to place impact sounds.
- **Lead the visual by ~10–20ms** (sound slightly BEFORE the motion begins) — the brain processes audio faster than visuals, so a tiny pre-cue makes the moment feel responsive/intentional.
- Pair animated graphics (text, icons, image pop-ups) with SFX so they feel integrated, not pasted on. Text pop = pop/ding.
- CTA close (last ~3s): a subtle **ding / positive-resolution sound** conditions viewers to associate the CTA with a positive signal.
- Whoosh = supports motion (camera moves, swipes, reveals, object passing through frame).

## 6. CAVEATS
- Many percentage claims (40% completion lift, 85% watch time lift from interrupts, 23–40% from styling) come from tool/agency blogs that have a product to sell. They are directionally consistent across sources but are not peer-reviewed. Use them to set sensible DEFAULTS, not as guarantees.
- Pixel safe-zone numbers are the most reliable (platform-UI driven). Reading-speed numbers (3–4 wps, 17 CPS, 42 chars) trace back to long-standing professional subtitling standards and are the safest to hard-code.
- "Dated vs current" is the fastest-moving axis — the Hormozi-preset-is-now-generic finding is the single most important aesthetic takeaway for differentiation in 2026.
